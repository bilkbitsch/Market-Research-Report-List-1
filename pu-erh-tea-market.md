<p><p>The Pu-erh tea market is primarily categorized into two types: raw (Sheng) Pu-erh and cooked (Shou) Pu-erh. Raw Pu-erh is made from naturally aged leaves, developing complex flavors over time, appealing to traditionalists and collectors. Conversely, cooked Pu-erh undergoes an accelerated fermentation process, producing a milder, earthy flavor that attracts casual drinkers. Both types cater to diverse consumer preferences, with raw Pu-erh often seen as an investment and cooked Pu-erh favored for its approachable taste and quicker availability.</p></p>
