<p><p>The anthocyanidin market consists of various types, each offering unique properties and applications. Delphinidin is known for its blue and purple hues, commonly found in blueberries and eggplants. Cyanidin, the most widely distributed anthocyanidin, imparts red to purple colors in fruits like cherries and pomegranates. Petunidin, associated with purple pigmentation in petals, is also popular in food and beverages. Peonidin provides red colors in foods like berries. The "Other" category includes lesser-known variants like malvidin, expanding market diversity.</p></p>
