<p><p>Photoactive compounds (PACs) are essential components in photoresists, which are used in photolithography processes for semiconductor manufacturing. The market for PACs is segmented into two main types: G-line photoresist, which operates at a wavelength of 436 nm, and I-line photoresist, functioning at a wavelength of 365 nm. G-line PACs are typically used for less demanding applications, while I-line PACs provide higher resolution and sensitivity, catering to advanced semiconductor fabrication needs. Both types play a pivotal role in the development of microelectronic devices.</p></p>
