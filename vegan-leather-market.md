<p><p>The vegan leather market comprises three primary types: </p><p>1. **Plant-based Textiles**: These innovative materials are derived from natural sources like pineapple leaves, apple peels, and mushrooms, offering biodegradable and sustainable alternatives to traditional leather.</p><p>2. **Polyurethane (PU)**: A synthetic option, PU offers a leather-like finish and is more environmentally friendly than traditional leather in terms of production processes, although its durability and biodegradability can vary.</p><p>3. **Polyvinyl Plastic (Vinyl)**: Known for its affordability and flexibility, vinyl vegan leather is widely used in fashion and upholstery, but it is less sustainable due to its petroleum-based origin and limited recyclability.</p></p>
