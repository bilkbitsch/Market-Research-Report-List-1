<p><p>High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) Transmission is a technology used to transmit electrical power over long distances with minimal energy loss. Unlike traditional alternating current (AC) systems, HVDC systems maintain a constant voltage level, allowing for efficient power transfer, particularly in underwater cables and across regions with significant geographical challenges. This technology is increasingly favored for connecting renewable energy sources, such as wind and solar power, to the grid, facilitating the integration of clean energy and reducing greenhouse gas emissions.</p><p>The High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) Transmission Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.3% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by rising energy demand, the need for grid interconnections, and the push for decarbonization in energy systems. Recent trends indicate a shift towards more advanced HVDC technologies, including voltage source converters (VSC), which offer enhanced flexibility and controllability. Countries are investing in HVDC infrastructure to optimize their energy networks, support electrification initiatives, and improve grid resilience. Additionally, the increasing focus on renewable energy resources is expected to further stimulate the demand for HVDC systems, positioning them as a critical component of future power transmission networks.</p></p>
